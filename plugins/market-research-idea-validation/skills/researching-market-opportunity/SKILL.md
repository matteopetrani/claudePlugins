# Researching Market Opportunity

## Overview

This skill quantifies your target market, identifies addressable segments, and validates market demand through data-driven analysis.

## Configuration

Read market segmentation from `domain-config.md`:
- Market category (for TAM)
- Market subcategory (for SAM)
- Target audience (for SOM)

## Evidence standards

- Cite sources for each market size or trend claim.
- Use this citation format: `[Source Name](URL) - accessed YYYY-MM-DD`

## Research process

1. **Gather market sizing data**:
   - Market category size and growth rates (from domain-config.md)
   - Market subcategory adoption and revenue trends
   - Key players and market consolidation
   - Regional variations and international markets

2. **Analyze addressable market**:
   - TAM: Total market for your category (global opportunity)
   - SAM: Serviceable addressable market (your subcategory/niche)
   - SOM: Serviceable obtainable market (realistic year 1-2 capture)

3. **Research market trends**:
   - Personalization and innovation trends
   - Monetization dynamics (subscription, freemium, etc.)
   - User acquisition and discovery channels
   - Geographic growth variations
   - Technology and feature evolution

If sources are inaccessible, ask the user for reports or links and proceed only with what is provided.

## Output format

Create `research/market-opportunity.md` with:
- Market sizing breakdown (TAM/SAM/SOM) with citations
- Growth trends and projections
- Market structure and key players
- Monetization landscape and pricing strategies
- Opportunities and strategic recommendations
- Explicit calculations and inputs for TAM/SAM/SOM (with currency and year)
- Source quality notes (primary vs secondary)
- Limits and unknowns

## Success criteria

- Market size estimates with credible sources
- Clear addressable niche definition
- Data supporting product-market fit
- Realistic revenue projections
- Evidence and citations included for key claims
- Documented limitations if data is sparse or unavailable
